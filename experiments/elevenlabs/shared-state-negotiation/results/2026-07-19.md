**PASS.** The experiment demonstrated state crossing two simultaneously active ElevenLabs conversations without the enterprise monitoring endpoint.

## Observed sequence

1. Atlas Freight conversation `conv_6201kxvqm0gpegprxg34tgar20es` submitted CHF 1,500 all-in through `demo_record_offer`; the hub became market version 1.
2. Bolt Logistics conversation `conv_1001kxvqm0gdebf9a9f2z5e4k83h` submitted CHF 1,650 all-in; the hub became market version 2.
3. The Bolt agent received Atlas's verified offer in the webhook result and replied: “I have a verified competing all-in offer of CHF 1'500 with terms: all-in, including fuel and tolls. Can you beat it?”
4. While both conversations remained active, Atlas revised its offer to CHF 1,400; the hub became market version 3.
5. Bolt invoked `demo_sync_market_state` and replied with the revised CHF 1,400 offer.

The final in-memory state contained Atlas at CHF 1,400 and Bolt at CHF 1,650, plus three `offer.recorded` events and a `market.synced` event.

## Interpretation boundary

This proves cross-conversation propagation at webhook/turn boundaries. It does not prove unsolicited mid-utterance context injection, PSTN outbound calling, production tunnel availability, or behavior under five-way concurrency.
